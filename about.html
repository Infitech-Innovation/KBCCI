About KBCCI

The Kenya–Benelux–EU Chamber of Commerce and Industry (KBCCI) is the first official Chamber of Commerce of Kenya in Europe, established in Brussels, Belgium — the heart of the EU and the Benelux region.

It was founded on 23 June 2025 in close collaboration with the Kenyan Mission in Brussels, under the leadership of H.E. Ambassador Bitange Ndemo and Irene Kiti.

KBCCI operates from its headquarters in Brussels, Belgium, serving as a gateway for trade, investment, and bilateral cooperation between Kenya and the Benelux region (Belgium, Netherlands, Luxembourg). The Chamber is a non-profit, membership-based organization.

Why KBCCI

The Chamber builds on a long-standing tradition of business cooperation and aims to provide a formal, credible, and sustainable platform for partnerships across sectors.

Its founding team includes professionals from Kenya and Europe, with strong support from the Kenyan Embassy in Belgium and key advisors with deep experience in innovation, diplomacy, business development, and institutional networks.

Vision

To be the leading platform that strengthens trade, investment, and diplomatic ties between Kenya and the Benelux/EU. We connect businesses and institutions to the right partners, provide strategic market intelligence, and foster trust, innovation, and sustainable cooperation. Through impactful networking, investment forums, and knowledge exchange, we aspire to unlock opportunities, deepen bilateral relations, and contribute to shared prosperity.

Mission

To empower and connect businesses in Kenya and the Benelux/EU by fostering trust, facilitating partnerships, and providing strategic support that drives sustainable trade, investment, and innovation.
